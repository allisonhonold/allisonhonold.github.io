WalkSafe

New York City Walk Safety Predictor App

This Flask app predicts and displays the rlative safety of walking based on historic arrest data, weather, and start and end location. It combines information intake from users, API calls, the machine learning model backend, and mapping of the results.

To achieve this end, I used Google Maps API and JavaScript, DarkSky API calls for up to date weather, MongoDB, SQL, a logistic regression model (chosen to maximize logloss within the project size constraint of 1 gb), GeoJSON and Shapely to work with the geospatial aspects of the data, and Folium mapping to present the results overlaid on a map for practial use.

Investigating Grading at UW Madison

Hypothesis testing, complex SQL querying

Aaron Chen and I used the UW Madison Courses and Grades 2006-2017 data set to investigate the following questions:

  • Does the day of the week a class is held affect average student success?
  • Have grades increased or decreased over time? Is there evidence of grade inflation or grade deflation?
  • Does the time of day a class is held affect student success?
  • Do individual teachers in the math department grade differently (easier or harder) than the deparment as a whole?
  • Do STEM classes have a lower section avearge GPA than non-stem classes?

In addition to querying the database with SQL, we utilized the following python packages and modules: sqlalchemy, pandas, matplotlib.pyplot, numpy, and scipy.stats.

Targeting Outreach and Support for Predicted Drug Users

Training Classification Models

In this project, I worked with a team of four to create the optimal Classification Models that would predict whether someone is a likely user of the substance of interest based on their demographics, personality, and use of other substances. We created models for each of the following substances: Heroin, Amphetamines, and Methamphetamine. These substances were chosen based on percieved impact on users and their communities, with the goal of getting the most impact from the outreach and support budgets.

To determine the best model for each substance, we looked to maximize the F-1 score as a balance between precision and recall, in an effort to identify most of the people in need of support (recall) while not spending too much resources where they are not needed (precision).

Our model search included the folowing machine learning model families: K-Nearest Neighbors Classifiers, Linear Support Vector Classifiers, Gaussian Proccess Classifiers, Decision Tree Classifiers, Random Forest CLassifiers, AdaBoost Classifiers, Gassuian Naieve Bayes Classifiers, and Quadratic Discriminant Analysis.

Identifying Zipcodes for Investment Properties

Maximize predicted ROI with time series analysis

Dirk Van Curan II and I used the Zillow Research data set to identify zipcodes that would fit our investment goals over the five years following our data set (April 2018). We were looking for the top five zipcodes for upside potential, expected value, and lower risk. We measured in terms of maximum ROI over the five year period, without regard to the time value of money, since we are using the same five year period for each potential investment.

We used fbProphet for our time series modeling, pandas for our data cleaning and dataframe handling, matplotlib.pyplot for our data visualizations, and scipy.stats to assist with our statistical analysis.
